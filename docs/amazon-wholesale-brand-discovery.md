# Finding Emerging Brands That Allow Amazon Reselling

A working playbook for adding 1–3 new wholesale brand relationships per month
to a multi-channel retail operation focused on Amazon FBA.

> **Why methodology, not a brand list:** Brand policies change quarterly.
> A brand that welcomed Amazon resellers last year may have just signed an
> exclusive with a different reseller this quarter. The skill is finding
> them continuously, not memorizing a static list.

---

## The core insight

Most established CPG brands (Unilever, P&G, Hasbro, Nike, Jellycat) actively
restrict Amazon authorized-reseller programs because they protect their own
direct presence on Amazon. Trying to get authorized as a small reseller for
these brands is mostly a dead end.

But for every restricted brand, there are **hundreds of emerging brands**
that are the opposite — they NEED Amazon distribution and don't have the
internal logistics to do FBA themselves. These brands actively recruit
resellers. They're just harder to find because they're smaller, newer, and
spread across thousands of categories.

The arithmetic that matters:
- Adding 1–2 brands/month at $1–3k/mo each in net revenue
- Compounded over 12 months = $15–35k/mo in additional revenue
- Margin on direct-from-brand wholesale: typically 30–50%

This is how $30k/mo Amazon arbitrage operations scale to $100k+/mo.

## Where Amazon-friendly emerging brands cluster

Higher concentration of "yes to Amazon" brands in these categories:

| Category | Why it works |
|---|---|
| **Niche supplements & vitamins** | Smaller brands, fragmented competition, FDA labeling already done |
| **Clean / indie beauty** | DTC-native brands without internal Amazon teams |
| **Specialty pet products** | Big growth category, many indie entrants |
| **Specialty diet food** (keto, paleo, gluten-free, allergen-free) | Smaller brands targeting Amazon's diet-shopper audience |
| **Home fragrance / candles** | Many small brands, low-overhead category |
| **Maker / craft / hobby supplies** | Long tail, often family-owned |
| **Tech accessories** (cables, chargers, mounts) | Many brands, low brand loyalty, rapid SKU turnover |
| **Outdoor / camping accessories** (specific gear, not Coleman-tier) | Emerging direct-to-consumer brands |
| **Kitchen gadgets / utensils** | Single-product brands often want resellers |
| **Stationery / planners / journals** | Indie brands, founder-led |
| **Toys & games (indie / educational)** | Non-Hasbro/Mattel toy brands often allow Amazon |

Lower concentration / mostly restricted:
- Mass-market H&B (Dove, VO5 territory)
- Major footwear & apparel
- Premium gift (Jellycat, Vera Bradley territory)
- Major confectionery
- Premium kitchenware (Le Creuset, etc.)

## The Discovery Funnel

A repeatable process for finding 5–10 candidate brands per week.

### Step 1: Mine Amazon search results

Pick a category you want to expand into. On Amazon, search a generic
keyword in that category (e.g. "magnesium glycinate," "cat dental treats,"
"travel journal").

Look at the results page and **filter for products with these signals**:

| Signal | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **5–15 third-party sellers on the listing** | Brand explicitly allows multi-seller distribution |
| **BSR between 5,000–100,000 in main category** | Real sales velocity, not too saturated |
| **Brand has its own Amazon storefront BUT doesn't dominate sales** | Brand likely welcomes resellers as growth channel |
| **Brand established < 5 years ago** | Less likely to have legacy exclusive distributor agreements |
| **Listing has 200+ reviews with 4+ stars** | Product actually sells and customers like it |
| **Brand website looks invested-in but not overproduced** | Founder-led, likely receptive to direct outreach |

❌ **Skip if you see**:
- Only the brand itself selling (no resellers) → channel-controlled
- 50+ sellers all undercutting → margin race to bottom
- BSR worse than 200,000 → too slow-moving
- Brand storefront looks corporate/big → unlikely to take small resellers
- "Sold by Amazon.com" appears → Amazon Vendor Central, no third-party

### Step 2: Verify product economics with Keepa

For each candidate, paste the ASIN into Keepa ([keepa.com](https://keepa.com)).
Check:

- **Sales rank graph** — consistent (good) vs erratic spikes (bad)
- **Buy box price history** — stable (margin protected) vs constantly dropping (race to bottom)
- **Number of FBA sellers over time** — growing modestly (healthy) vs exploding (over-saturated)
- **Out-of-stock periods** — frequent? = supply constrained, brand might want more sellers

You want products where the **price floor has held steady for 6+ months**
and there are **3–10 active FBA sellers**, not 50.

### Step 3: Find the brand website + contact

From the Amazon listing:
1. Click brand name → brand storefront
2. Brand storefront sometimes shows their domain
3. If not, Google: `"[Brand Name]" wholesale OR trade OR reseller`
4. Look at brand website footer for: Wholesale, Trade, Stockists, B2B, For Retailers

Find a real human contact:
- **Brand website footer** for `wholesale@brand.com`-type address
- **LinkedIn search**: `"[Brand Name]" sales OR wholesale OR partnerships`
- **Founder name + email pattern** (firstname@brand.com, firstname.lastname@brand.com)
- **Hunter.io** or **Apollo.io** can find email patterns from a domain

### Step 4: Send the qualifying email

Use this short-form first contact (different from the longer Tier-1 templates):

**Subject:** Wholesale inquiry — Pernikus LLC, FL-based multi-channel CPG retailer

```
Hi [Name],

I run Pernikus LLC, a Florida-based multi-channel retailer of consumer
packaged goods. We move a meaningful volume on Amazon FBA, our direct
e-commerce site (pernikuswholesale.com), and a small B2B network.

I came across [Brand] through [where you found them] and we'd like to
explore carrying [specific 1-3 SKUs by name].

Three quick qualifying questions before going deeper:

  1. Do you sell wholesale to multi-channel retailers, including Amazon?
  2. If yes, what is your wholesale pricing structure and minimum opening
     order?
  3. Do you have an authorized-seller or MAP policy we should be aware of?

For credentials: EIN-verified Florida LLC since 2023, active FL Annual
Resale Certificate, D-U-N-S 041-702-526. Public credentials reference
at pernikuswholesale.com/credentials.

Whatever the answer is, I appreciate the clarity — happy to move forward
or just take a "not a fit" if Amazon isn't part of your distribution
strategy.

Thanks,
Ricardo Siberio
Pernikus LLC
ricardo@pernikuswholesale.com  •  (407) 881-7996
```

The three-questions-up-front structure is critical. It:
1. Forces a yes/no answer (high response rate)
2. Filters out brands that won't allow Amazon (no time wasted)
3. Surfaces MAP/authorization requirements before you're emotionally invested

Expected response rate: 30–50% on cold emails to qualifying brands.

### Step 5: Vet the response carefully

When a brand says yes, ask for **before placing any order**:

- ✅ Wholesale price list (not "we'll work it out")
- ✅ Written confirmation Amazon FBA reselling is permitted
- ✅ MAP policy in writing
- ✅ Minimum opening order + reorder requirements
- ✅ Whether they file complaints against unauthorized sellers (a healthy
     sign — means brand-gated category protection)

If they're cagey on any of these, walk away. Real wholesale relationships
have all five documented.

### Step 6: Test before committing

First order from any new brand:
- Order **just enough** to send 5–20 units to FBA
- Watch for **30 days** of sell-through and price stability
- Check the listing weekly for new sellers undercutting
- If margin holds and velocity is real, scale up the next order 5–10×
- If it doesn't, no big loss

## Where to find brand-discovery leads in bulk

### Communities (active, free)

- **r/FulfillmentByAmazon** on Reddit — sellers share leads, scams, brand updates
- **r/AmazonSeller** — broader, less wholesale-focused but useful
- **Facebook groups**: "Amazon Wholesale Sellers" (large, active)
- **Discord servers**: search "Amazon FBA" — multiple active communities

### Marketplaces / databases

- **RangeMe** ([rangeme.com](https://rangeme.com)) — emerging brands looking for retailers
- **Wholesale Central** ([wholesalecentral.com](https://wholesalecentral.com)) — free directory
- **Salehoo** — paid wholesale directory
- **Faire / Tundra / Abound** — useful for *discovery* even if you can't sell most on Amazon (you'll spot brands and then approach them direct, off-platform)

### Trade shows (worth a yearly trip)

- **ASD Market Week** (Las Vegas, March + August) — single best US trade show for general consumer goods + emerging brands explicitly seeking online retailers
- **NY NOW** (gift / lifestyle, twice yearly)
- **Natural Products Expo East/West** (natural & specialty CPG)
- **Toy Fair** (NYC, February)

A single ASD trip can yield 5–15 new brand relationships if you go prepared
with business cards, your one-pager, and a list of categories you want.

### Paid education (only if you want it)

- **The Wholesale Formula** — well-known course teaching this exact method
  ($3,000+ — only buy if your operation is at $50k+/mo and you want to scale
  systematically). Most of the methodology is freely available; the course
  is mostly community + accountability.

Goal: contact 10–20 brands per month, convert 1–3 to Active, scale 1 per
quarter into a meaningful (>$2k/mo) revenue contributor.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

1. **Don't bulk-buy from a new brand without verifying Amazon permission in
   writing.** A brand that approves you verbally and later complains to
   Amazon will get your account suspended for unauthorized selling.

2. **Don't compete on price within the buy box on a brand you don't have
   exclusivity-ish with.** If 8 sellers are racing each other to the bottom,
   the brand isn't protecting margin and the relationship will eventually
   die. Walk away.

3. **Don't tell brands "we'll do anything you want" to get approved.** They
   want disciplined channel partners, not desperate ones. Walk in with
   credentials and confidence; ask qualifying questions.

4. **Don't forget the brand needs you too.** Emerging brands need
   resellers more than the resellers need them. You're not begging.

5. **Don't waste time on brands that haven't responded in 3 weeks.** Move
   on. Re-engage in 90 days if relevant.

## Reasonable goals

- **Month 1**: 20 brands contacted, 4–6 responses, 1–2 wholesale price
  lists received, 0–1 test orders placed
- **Month 3**: 1–2 brands actively reordering at meaningful volume
- **Month 6**: 4–6 active brands, 1 contributing >$2k/mo
- **Month 12**: 8–15 active brands, 3–5 contributing >$2k/mo each, total
  $20–40k/mo in net-new revenue from this channel

This compounds. By year 2, you have an outreach machine that produces
new brand relationships continuously, your catalog is diversified away
from over-restricted mass brands, and your business is more defensible.
